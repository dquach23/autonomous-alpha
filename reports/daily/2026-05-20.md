### 🔴 #1 — PLTR (Palantir Technologies) | ~$133 | Fwd P/E: ~97x


As of May 15, Palantir shares had dropped 26% year-to-date, trading around $133.71 — roughly $30 below their March highs — despite the company posting 85% year-over-year revenue growth and a Rule of 40 score of 145%.
 This is the textbook definition of priced-for-perfection: the market is not rewarding exceptional growth because it was already in the price.

**The risk:** 
The post-earnings selloff reflects valuation compression at 97x forward earnings. The stock needs to prove growth rates will hold or increase even as the revenue base expands — any deceleration will be punished severely.


**The structural problem:** 
While commercial growth is accelerating, its sequential momentum has been uneven, with new contract value (TCV) growth slowing — suggesting greater dependence on existing government bookings.
 Any sign of a federal budget squeeze (DOGE-related cuts, continuing resolutions) is a hard negative catalyst.

**Drawdown scenario:** 
PLTR is priced not just for sustained growth but for sustained *extraordinary* growth. Shares could get crushed if any evidence of a material slowdown surfaces.
 A re-rate from 97x to 50x forward earnings = ~50% drawdown even on flat numbers.
